About Me

I am a Geospatial Data Analyst with a dynamic career spanning four years. Currently serving as the Geospatial IT Manager/Analyst at ENPLAN since November 2021, I excel in orchestrating data automation for diverse datasets and leading end-to-end product development. My expertise includes creating and optimizing ETL processes for disperate datasets, creating robust business metrics pipelines, and designing product tools and data systems. I also engineer custom outreach platforms using web scraping and data cleaning techniques, manage IT operations, and oversee extensive email marketing campaigns.

Prior to ENPLAN, I contributed as a General Assistant IT - GIS at the County of Shasta from March 2020 to May 2021. In this role, I cleaned and updated geospatial data for county web maps, collaborating seamlessly with county departments to meet project requirements.

My educational background comprises a B.S. in Business Information Systems from Chico State University, an A.S. in Geographic Information System from Shasta College, Redding, and an A.A. in Quantitative Reasoning from Shasta College. With a strong foundation in project management, planning, and stakeholder engagement, I bring a holistic approach to problem-solving and continual learning in every endeavor.

Metrics Pipeline and Reporting

The primary goal of this project was to establish a comprehensive Metrics Pipeline and Reporting system to gain insights into user interactions with our web maps. To achieve this, I designed and implemented a user logging schema within our PostgreSQL database. The project seamlessly integrated SQL, Python, Pandas, NumPy, and Matplotlib to analyze data, generate meaningful metrics, and present them through visually informative graphs.
